VR

Virtual Reality

Virtual reality (VR) is a simulated experience that uses position tracking and 3D near-eye displays to immerse the viewer in a virtual world. Virtual reality has applications in business, education (such as medical or military training), and entertainment (especially video games) (such as virtual meetings). Aside from virtual reality, other different forms of technology include augmented reality and mixed reality, sometimes known as extended reality or XR, however definitions are still evolving as the field develops.

About VR

Introdution

Virtual reality was foreshadowed by the discovery of perspective in Renaissance European painting and the stereoscope. Science fiction was the first genre to make use of the more recent concept of virtual reality. In 1962, Morton Heilig created a prototype of his idea known as the Sensorama. At NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), David Em made history as the first artist to create navigable virtual worlds between 1977 and 1984. Consumer headsets first became widely available on the commercial market in the 1990s. For example, Computer Gaming World stated "cheap VR by 1994" in 1992. Sega introduced the Sega VR headgear for the Mega Drive home gaming system in 1991. The system tracked and responded to the movements of the user's head using inertial sensors, stereo headphones, and LCD panels in the visor.
